About me

Aspiring researcher. At the moment working on the use of graphical methods for model selection in high-dimensional settings. I am interested in economics methodology, especially the use of DAGs, but also more generally in causality and how it is understood in science. Find some of my previous projects in econometrics and data science underneath. I also write opinion pieces on Medium.

Causal Models and External Validity of Randomized Experiments
This paper evaluates from a philosophical perspective how to use of experimentally evaluated causal claims in practice to inform policy. It argues that often external validity is not sufficiently accounted for in practice. Further, it suggests a theoretical framework with which causal claims can be adjusted to different environments using observational data.

Collective Mood-States and Investment Behaviour during a Global Pandemic
In this paper, we investigated whether collective mood, triggered by a real event, has some influence on stock prices considering a causal structure that defines the mechanisms by which collective mood affects stock prices.

Model Selection with Graphical Considerations
I develop a novel testing-based model-selection method that makes use of graphical structure learning. Further, I analyse it in its application for post-double selection in high-dimensional problems.

The Effects of Universal Basic Income on Labour Force Participation - A Meta-Analysis
Among the potential benefits of UBI are the reduction in income inequality and the promise to leverage many out of poverty as well as creating a more inclusive labour market and improving gender equity. However, a voiced criticism is that it could disincentivise work. We conduct a meta-analysis with existing experimental data of UBI-like policies on labour market participation (LMP).
